# 1         Purpose

This document aims to establish Huawei's general policy for cyber security governance to guide cyber security of related business departments. It guides business departments in establishing and continuously improving cyber security policies, integrating cyber security policy requirements into related processes (including engineering, IT, and tool support), and enhancing cyber security organization and competence development, so as to ensure cyber security compliance, effective risk control, and competitiveness of Huawei's products, solutions, services, and operations, thereby supporting the achievement of Huawei's business objectives.

# 2         Applicability

This document applies to Huawei Investment & Holding Co., Ltd. and its wholly-owned subsidiaries under its direct or indirect control worldwide (hereinafter referred to as "Huawei" or "the Company").

# 3         Huawei's Principles for Cyber Security Governance

Cyber security and privacy protection are Huawei's top priorities. Huawei adheres to the cyber security and privacy protection values of integrity, trustworthiness, capability, accountability, openness, and transparency. On this basis, Huawei builds trustworthiness capabilities of products and solutions, and works with the industry chain to support and ensure cyber security and privacy of customers and Huawei-operated businesses. Huawei complies with the following principles for cyber security governance:

## 3.1        **Security-oriented:** 
In Huawei, supporting and ensuring cyber security and privacy of customers and Huawei-operated businesses is a basic business requirement. Huawei ensures that sufficient resources are invested in meeting cyber security and privacy protection requirements.

## 3.2        **Legal and regulatory compliance:** 
Huawei complies with applicable cyber security laws and regulations of countries and regions.

## 3.3        **Integration into business:** 
Huawei integrates cyber security assurance activities into the policies, processes, specifications, and baselines of related businesses, and provides secure and trustworthy products, solutions, and services for customers, so as to guarantee business success.

## 3.4        **Director accountability:** 
Directors of business departments at all levels are the primary owners for ensuring cyber security within their businesses. Process owners at all levels take first-person responsibility for ensuring cyber security throughout the processes under their charge.

## 3.5        **Engagement of all staff:** 
All employees are aware of the importance of cyber security, have related capabilities, and implement cyber security requirements in practice. Every one shall be technically and legally accountable for what they do and for the consequences of their actions.

## 3.6        **Independent verification:** 
Trust needs to be based on facts, facts must be verifiable, and verification must be based on common standards. On this basis, Huawei performs independent evaluation and verification by layer following the ABC principle — assume nothing, believe no one, check everything.

## 3.7        **Openness and cooperation:** 
Being open and transparent, Huawei sincerely and actively communicates and cooperates with stakeholders, such as customers, suppliers, partners, and industry organizations, to implement the concepts of "shared responsibility, capability co-construction, and value sharing" so as to address cyber security threats and challenges.

## 3.8        **Continuous improvement:** 
Cyber security is a continuous risk management and capability building process. There is no absolute security or once-for-all solution. We need to promptly review weaknesses and continuously strengthen the suitability, adequacy, and effectiveness of cyber security management and technical measures.

# 4         General Cyber Security Policy

Huawei's general policy for cyber security governance has been developed based on the insights into applicable cyber security laws, regulations, and standards, mainstream cyber security governance and management standards, as well as Huawei's practices. For the general policy, the L1 and L2 are the framework. High-level requirements of cyber security governance are at L3 and are detailed in the attachment. The attachment is maintained and updated by GSPO Office. Each domain shall refine and adapt the high-level requirements as required in order to guide business departments in implementation.

## 4.1        **Strategy and risk management:** 
Gain insights into the internal and external environments of the organization, and understand cyber security laws and regulations, contractual obligations, and requirements and expectations of other stakeholders. Establish the cyber security governance framework and cyber security strategy, develop and update the cyber security policy, and provide guidance on the cyber security strategy and risk management.

## 4.2        **Assurance and continuous improvement:** 
Develop an end-to-end evaluation and verification framework for cyber security and privacy protection to monitor and evaluate the implementation of cyber security requirements. Establish an independent multi-party evaluation and verification mechanism, continuously verify the suitability, adequacy, and effectiveness of management and technical measures based on specified objective standards, and provide evidence-based evaluation, verification, inspection, and audit results of products, solutions, Huawei-operated businesses, management systems, and personnel. Make constant efforts to improve Huawei's cyber security assurance system to enhance the confidence of internal and external stakeholders.

## 4.3        **Communication and cooperation:**

Carry out two-way communication with stakeholders to convey key information about Huawei's cyber security governance and business practices and in turn learn stakeholders' concerns and expectations, so as to strengthen cooperation. Proactively define and build Huawei's brand and image of cyber security and privacy protection.

## 4.4        **Privacy protection:** 
Process personal data in compliance with applicable laws and regulations following the basic principles of "lawfulness, fairness, and transparency; purpose limitation; data minimization; accuracy; storage period limitation; integrity and confidentiality; and accountability".

## 4.5        **Product and solution security:** 
Ensure that related personnel, processes, engineering, and technologies are ready for product security development at the product team level. Develop well-secured products, protect product integrity, actively contribute to industry standards, and cooperate with the ecosystem. Help customers build cyber resilience and mitigate cyber risks.

## 4.6        **Marketing security:** 
Establish a marketing campaign management mechanism that meets cyber security laws and regulations and Huawei's requirements and covers product sales control, contract commitment management, and lifecycle management, to ensure security compliance and create value for customers.

## 4.7        **Supply chain security:** 
Develop supply chain-oriented security management rules. Promote suppliers and partners to improve security and trustworthiness capabilities, to comply with related security standards, and to meet the same security requirements as that Huawei shall meet. Strengthen integrity protection measures for product introduction, manufacturing, and distribution to prevent products from being tampered with, implanted with malware, or forged, and ensure the integrity, authenticity, and traceability of products in the supply chain.

## 4.8        **Delivery and service security:** 
Manage compliance and security risks based on customer authorization when providing products and services for customers. Establish an end-to-end vulnerability management mechanism to support and ensure network security and stability.

## 4.9        **Huawei-operated business security:** 
Integrate security into continuous development and O&M (DevSecOps) to ensure the security of the development process, product, and running of cloud services. Work with ecosystem partners and developers to build an open security ecosystem. Establish a mechanism for operations qualification management and business security risk control based on applicable laws and regulations, and specify responsibility boundaries to ensure security of business operations.

## 4.10    **Organization and personnel:** 
Provide fundamental support in terms of organizations and personnel for establishing the end-to-end cyber security assurance system. By clearly defining organizations, roles, and responsibilities and providing necessary resources, enable employees to strengthen cyber security awareness and obtain necessary expertise, and facilitate the success of cyber security work.

## 4.11    **IT platform and O&M security:** 
Securing the tools, IT systems, and infrastructure on which business activities depend is the basis for cyber security work. Threats and security incidents shall be continuously and dynamically managed based on the IPDRR methodology — Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, and Recover. Identify data assets and compliance requirements, register and take stock of data assets, specify data security owners, and manage data security throughout the data lifecycle. Ensure that Huawei's operations comply with regulations and contracts, and cyber security risks are controllable. Build data security capabilities of products and Huawei-operated systems, and support and enable data compliance and security assurance during customers' use of products or system running, and promote the realization of data value.

# 5         Organization and Responsibility

5.1        The Global Cyber Security and User Privacy Protection Committee (GSPC) is Huawei's governance and decision-making organization in the cyber security and privacy protection field. It is responsible for making decisions on and approving the corporate cyber security and privacy protection strategies and policies, and managing cyber security and privacy compliance and risks.

5.2        Cyber security and privacy protection technical management committees of ICT, CBG, and other domains are responsible for building secure, trustworthy, and high-quality products and solutions, implementing cyber security and privacy protection engineering and technologies, building cyber security and privacy protection technical capabilities, and managing competitiveness goals. They make investment decisions on related products, solutions, platforms, and technologies, amd take responsibility for implementing cyber security and privacy protection technologies in Huawei products, building industry standards and ecosystem, controlling risks, and developing organizations, talent, and culture.

5.3        Directors of business departments at all levels are the primary owners for ensuring cyber security within businesses under their charge, and process owners at all levels take first-person responsibility for ensuring cyber security throughout the processes under their charge. They are responsible for implementing the cyber security policies. Directors in BGs, BUs, domain-specific subsidiaries, and related business departments are responsible for all operational results, including technical competitiveness, market share, financial profit and loss, and liable for violations of cyber security laws. In addition, they are responsible for building professional security teams and capabilities in their domains.

5.4        Regional presidents are responsible for the final regional cyber security and privacy results, and rep office/country GMs are responsible for the final local cyber security and privacy results. They conduct coordinated operations through the Regional Cyber Security & Privacy Committee (RSPC) and Country Cyber Security & Privacy Committee (CSPC) respectively.

5.5        Global Cyber Security & Privacy Officer (GSPO)/GSPO Office is responsible for supporting the GSPC's operation, helping the GSPC develop the general cyber security and privacy protection policy, and maintaining and promoting the general policy. It plans, develops, and maintains corporate-level cyber security and privacy protection policies based on the general policy, incorporates the GSPC and other requirements into the cyber security governance process, and evaluates, verifies, measures, inspects, and supervises the implementation. It acts as the risk control owner (RCO) for cyber security and privacy protection. It is tasked to maintain professional cyber security and privacy protection organizations, improve their professionalism and stability, support the operation of the Cyber Security and Privacy Protection Governance Profession Committee, and guide the capability improvement of professional teams. Internal Cyber Security Lab (ICSL) is responsible for independent evaluation and verification. The evaluation and verification result is a basis for GSPO to exercise the right of veto against product versions.

5.6        Cyber security and privacy protection business departments/offices at all levels are responsible for planning, developing, and maintaining domain-specific cyber security policies based on the general policy, integrating related requirements into relevant business processes, and monitoring and evaluating the implementation.

5.7        Referring to this document, Regional/Country Cyber Security & Privacy Officers (RSPOs/CSPOs) develop and maintain local cyber security policies in compliance with local applicable laws and regulations, incorporate relevant requirements into local business processes, and monitor and evaluate the implementation. As local cyber security and privacy protection RCOs, RSPOs/CSPOs develop cyber security and privacy protection capabilities internally and build trust externally while ensuring business compliance, in order to ensure the success of local business. RSPOs/CSPOs assist in professional cyber security and privacy protection communication. They communicate with cyber security and privacy protection stakeholders from technologies, standards, laws and regulations, and other aspects based on business needs.

5.8        Legal Affairs Dept is the legal competence center for cyber security and privacy protection. It is responsible for following up on and analyzing global cyber security and privacy protection laws and regulations, helping business departments develop compliance requirements, and providing methods for cyber security and privacy compliance.

5.9        Human resource departments at all levels are responsible for providing solutions to support cyber security and privacy protection from both business and human resource perspectives, and making cyber security and privacy protection positions, capabilities, and appraisals explicit. They shall raise all-staff cyber security awareness, and support business departments in improving the skills of cyber security practitioners so as to effectively support business success.

5.10    As the corporate-level technology competence center of cyber security and privacy protection, 2012 Labs is responsible for improving and accumulating cyber security engineering and technical capabilities, developing product-related cyber security technical standards and specifications, and enabling R&D organizations. Under the GSPC's authorization and GSPO's guidance, Vulnerability Management Center manages and supervises vulnerabilities and makes reports to the GSPC. The Center exercises vulnerability management compliance and risk control duties on behalf of GSPO Office. BGs/BUs/Regions manage vulnerabilities in the procurement, supply, R&D, marketing, sales, service, and maintenance processes to ensure orderly process execution. Vulnerability Management Center develops basic vulnerability management capabilities on behalf of the Group, fully collaborates with corporate competence centers, such as 2012 Labs (Trustworthiness Theory, Technology & Engineering Lab), to provide platform support, and enables BGs, BUs, and domain-specific subsidiaries to support mitigation of vulnerability risks on their customers' live networks. Open Source Management Center is responsible for breaking down the corporate cyber security requirements and open source and third-party software management policies, developing cyber security management requirements for open source and third-party software, and integrating the requirements into related management mechanisms and processes for implementation.

5.11    R&D and service organizations (such as SPDTs and SDTs) are responsible for developing and delivering well-secured products, solutions, and services to help customers build cyber resilience and mitigate cyber risks.

 5.12    Organizations related to Huawei-operated businesses are responsible for building security development and security O&M capabilities of products, thereby ensuring security compliance of business operations.

 5.13    Marketing-related organizations are responsible for managing product sales and sensitive projects, ensuring compliance of marketing campaigns, and communicating and cooperating with customers.

 5.14    Procurement Dept, Manufacturing Dept, and Supply Chain Mgmt Dept are responsible for securing products in the supply chain, conveying cyber security responsibilities and requirements to suppliers and partners, and driving suppliers to improve their level of cyber security protection.

 5.15    Quality, Business Process & IT Dept provides common cyber security capabilities and platforms based on industry standards, industry best practices, and the IT intranet to ensure secure running of IT systems and applications related to cyber security.

 5.16    PACD communicates with government departments and the media to build a positive policy and public environment.

 5.17    Internal Audit Dept independently audits the compliance with cyber security requirements in each domain.

# 6         Exceptions and Deviations

Domain-specific subsidiaries shall adapt the general policy to develop their own cyber security policies. Any exception and deviation from the document shall be submitted to GSPO Office for evaluation and if necessary, submitted to GSPC for approval.
