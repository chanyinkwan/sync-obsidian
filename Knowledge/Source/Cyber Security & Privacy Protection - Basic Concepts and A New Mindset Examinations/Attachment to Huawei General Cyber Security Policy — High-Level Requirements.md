
# 1         Strategy and Risk Management

## 1.1        Insights into Cyber Security

·       **Understand the organization and its context**: Determine internal and external issues that are relevant to cyber security governance and that affect the organization's ability to achieve its objectives. Identify major risks and opportunities.

·       **Understand the needs and expectations of stakeholders:** Determine cyber security stakeholders and their cyber security requirements which may include legal and regulatory requirements and contractual obligations.

## 1.2        Governance Framework and Strategy

·       **Establish a cyber security governance framework:** Establish a cyber security governance framework under the guidance and supervision of the Board of Directors (BOD) to demonstrate the corporate management's leadership and commitment with respect to cyber security.

·       **Develop a cyber security strategy:** Develop a cyber security strategy that is supported, signed, and regularly reviewed by the BOD or its delegated organization GSPC, and ensure that the cyber security strategy is consistent with business strategies and can cope with identified major risks and opportunities.

## 1.3        Cyber Security Policy

·       **Develop a cyber security policy:** Develop a cyber security policy that is approved by the corporate management, and release it to all employees.

·       **Review and update the cyber security policy:** Review and update the cyber security policy on a regular basis or upon major changes to ensure that the policy is constantly suitable, adequate, and effective.

## 1.4        Compliance and Risk Management

·       **Specify compliance requirements:** Identify applicable legal and regulatory requirements, standards requirements, and contract compliance obligations, develop clear compliance methods, and strictly implement them.

·       **Develop a risk management mechanism:** Specify the policy requirements, methods, and guidelines for internal cyber security risk management. Establish a risk management framework and integrate risk management into related activities. Develop a risk management process and regularly carry out risk management.

## 1.5        Cyber Security Incident Management

·       **Establish an incident management mechanism:** Establish a hierarchical cyber security crisis management mechanism and specify management responsibilities and processes, to ensure prompt, effective, and orderly response to cyber security incidents.

·       **Prepare for incident handling:** Develop a cyber security crisis management plan (CMP) and perform routine drills.

·       **Report crises/incidents:** Identify and report existing or potential cyber security incidents as soon as possible through proper management methods.

·       **Respond to incidents:** Respond to cyber security incidents in accordance with the specified mechanism and process.

# 2         Assurance and Continuous Improvement

## 2.1        Evaluation and Verification System

·       **Establish an evaluation and verification mechanism**: Establish an independent multi-party evaluation and verification mechanism, continuously evaluate and verify the suitability, adequacy, and effectiveness of management and technical measures based on standards, provide fact-based verification results for products and solutions, Huawei-operated businesses, management systems, and personnel, and manage the process and results through the IT platform, to enhance the confidence of internal and external stakeholders.

·       **Check and testing:** Constantly perform cyber security and privacy protection checks and testing throughout the lifecycle to safeguard security and privacy. A version is subject to independent testing or evaluation before release, and the veto right (NO GO) can be exercised on the version based on the testing or evaluation results.

·       **Measurement and evaluation:** Establish a cyber security and privacy protection measurement and evaluation mechanism and regularly conduct measurement and management maturity evaluation to evaluate the effectiveness of cyber security process activities and continuously identify the areas of improvement.

·       **Inspection and audit:** Plan, establish, and conduct inspection and audit as scheduled to evaluate the effectiveness of cyber security work and ensure compliance with internal and external requirements.

·       **Verification by customers:** Provide resources and technical assistance based on customers' verification requirements and processes, support customers in verification, and manage verification results.

·       **Third-party verification:** Proactively plan and arrange independent third-party organizations to perform cyber security and privacy protection verification on Huawei's products and solutions, Huawei-operated businesses, management systems, and personnel in accordance with national or industry standards.

·       **Closed-loop management:** Implement closed-loop management of verification results, continuously improve the security and privacy quality of products, solutions, and Huawei-operated businesses, and enhance the capabilities of management systems and personnel.

## 2.2        Continuous Improvement

·       **Promptly correct nonconformities and continuously improve management:** React to any identified nonconformity and promptly correct it. Continuously improve the suitability, adequacy, and effectiveness of the cyber security governance system by learning from the nonconformities through root cause analysis, etc.

# 3         Communication and Cooperation

## 3.1        External Communication

·       **Build communication content and platforms:** Develop high-quality communication content based on end-to-end (E2E) cyber security and privacy practices. Provide online platforms such as the transparency center for communication at all regions anytime.

·       **Communicate with stakeholders:** Communicate with stakeholders in an open, transparent, and responsible manner, and share cyber security and privacy practices to help them address cyber security and privacy challenges and increase their confidence in Huawei's cyber security and privacy protection work.

## 3.2        External Cooperation

·       **External cooperation:** In cyber security and privacy protection domains, establish a mechanism of shared responsibility, capacity co-construction, and value sharing with stakeholders by means of joint technical innovation, technical evaluation and verification, talent development and enablement, standards adoption and promotion, etc.

## 3.3        Cyber Security and Privacy Protection Branding

·       **Brand and image:** Proactively define and build Huawei's brand and image of cyber security and privacy protection.

# 5         Product and Solution Security

## 5.1        Organization Preparation

·       **Develop security engineering specifications:** Continuously develop and improve security engineering specifications by analyzing cyber security laws/regulations, standards, best practices, and cases, to provide constraints and guidance for product versions and development activities.

·       **Build R&D support tool chains:** Continuously build and use automated R&D tools to improve the R&D efficiency and SDL security engineering quality, including but not limited to R&D work desktops and test tool platforms.

## 5.2        Development of Well-Secured Products

·       **Determine security objectives:** To effectively manage the cyber security risks of product versions, identify cyber security risks and determine the security objectives of product versions based on external requirements (such as applicable laws/regulations, regulatory requirements, standards, and best practices), internal requirements (such as organization policies, business objectives, and risk management policies), and product business scenarios.

·       **Plan functional requirements and assurance requirements for product security:** Based on the security objectives of product versions, determine the list of security functional requirements to be implemented and assurance requirements to be executed during R&D, so as to minimize the possibility of introducing product vulnerabilities and reduce security risks.

·       **Design products that meet security requirements:** Identify what security risks the product is likely to face throughout the product lifecycle and mitigate them by design. Determine the security controls that need to be implemented based on the risk decision-making process and prioritize standardized security controls in products.

·       **Review the product design to verify compliance with security requirements:** Check that the software and hardware design meets security requirements and risk controls, and that identified risks have been addressed.

·       **Manage security risks in the use of open source and third-party software:** Define security requirements for open source and third-party software. If open source and third-party software does not meet security requirements, establish and comply with related processes and mitigate the resulting risks. The selected open source and third-party software must comply with vulnerability management and product software lifecycle management requirements.

·       **Maximize reuse of existing secure and reliable software:** Reusing existing secure and reliable software helps lower the costs of software development, improve efficiency, and decrease the likelihood of introducing additional security vulnerabilities into the software.

·       **Create source code adhering to secure coding practices:** Coding based on best secure coding practices helps decrease the likelihood of introducing security vulnerabilities into the software.

·       **Review and analyze code to verify compliance with security requirements:** Review and analyze code to identify security defects and vulnerabilities and verify compliance with security requirements.

·       **Improve the security of executable code by using compiler security options:** Use compiler security options to decrease the likelihood of vulnerability exploitation.

·       **Perform security testing to verify compliance with security requirements:** Test the product to identify security defects and vulnerabilities, verify compliance with security requirements, and use automated methods for higher efficiency.

·       **Conduct independent security verification:** Conduct verification independent of the business system in accordance with Huawei's specifications and industry best practices, and ensure closed-loop management of Huawei's product security baseline requirements.

·       **Respond to and handle security issues:** Analyze the failure mechanism of security issues, find root causes for improvement, prevent the recurrence of such issues or similar issues, and verify and handle known security issues and vulnerabilities.

## 5.3        Protection of Product Integrity

·       **Provide an integrity verification mechanism:** This mechanism helps users verify the integrity of obtained software.

## 5.4        Resilience and Competitiveness Building

·       **Build resilience and competitiveness to help customers improve security operations:** Continuously enhance the resilience of products, solutions, and services, and help customers quickly detect and respond to security issues and promptly restore security operations.

## 5.5        Security Standards, Ecosystem, and Cooperation

·       **Standards compliance and certification:** Continuously identify regulatory and standards requirements and develop internal specifications to guide product compliance. Proactively plan and obtain third-party certifications recognized by the industry based on regulatory and customer requirements.

·       **Contribution to industry standards:** Participate in and contribute excellent practices to the development of cyber security standards in the industry, and work with industry stakeholders to promote the development of unified, industry-recognized standards.

·       **Security ecosystem and cooperation:** Gain insights into the industry security technologies and ecosystem, carry out cooperation in security technologies and products, develop an ecosystem for vulnerability and cyber security threat response, and improve security governance in open source communities.

# 6         Marketing Security

## 6.1        Security Sales

·       **Manage sales based on the saleable product list:** Develop a saleable product list in accordance with external laws and regulations and internal requirements, and manage product sales based on the list.

·       **Manage sensitive projects:** Determine whether a sales project is sensitive based on the customer and application domain. Sensitive projects shall be subject to a stricter approval and management mechanism.

·       **Manage marketing campaign security:** Marketing campaigns must comply with applicable cyber security laws and regulations as well as Huawei's management requirements.

·       **Manage contract commitments and lifecycle:** Incorporate cyber security commitments and lifecycle management clauses into contracts with customers, and manage the risks of products whose service life has expired on live networks.

·       **Manage consumer-oriented retail:** Comb through cyber security risks based on the lifecycle of retail services, implement risk mitigations, and ensure secure management and legitimate use of retail data.

## 6.2        Partner Management

·       **Security clauses in partner agreements/policies:** Manage the compliance of channel partners by applying partner policies. Specify in the partner agreement that partners shall comply with applicable laws and regulations as well as Huawei's general cyber security and privacy protection requirements for partners. In addition, specify their security responsibilities to ensure security and compliance.

·       **Security capability transfer:** Transfer Huawei's cyber security capabilities to key partners, enhance partners' confidence/trust in Huawei's cyber security, encourage partners to endorse Huawei, and communicate Huawei's cyber security specifications and operation guides to key partners.

# 7         Supply Chain Security

## 7.1        Supplier Security Management

·       **Supplier security system qualification:** Sign the Cyber Security Agreement, Non-Disclosure Agreement, and Data Processing Agreement with suppliers, and conduct cyber security system qualification, information security system qualification, and privacy protection system qualification on suppliers.

·       **Supplier material security qualification:** Enhance cyber security requirements in the requirement specifications of purchased materials and perform cyber security testing on purchased materials.

·       **Routine supplier security management:** Annually evaluate suppliers' security risks and security performance, and perform security inspections on suppliers based on the risk evaluation results.

·       **Business security management of business partners:** Business partners such as Electronic Manufacturing Services (EMS) suppliers, warehousing service providers, software service providers, service subcontractors, transportation service providers, and scrapping service suppliers shall comply with Huawei's cyber security and data protection agreements and requirements, prevent products from being tampered with, implanted with malware, or forged during R&D, production, logistics, service, and reuse, and ensure the traceability of processes.

·       **Supplier vulnerability management:** Sign the Service Level Agreement (SLA) and Vulnerability Response SLA with suppliers, and establish an emergency response mechanism to address supplier-side vulnerabilities.

## 7.2        Manufacturing Security

·       **Product manufacturing security:** Perform security control on software, hardware, ports, and data during product manufacturing design and introduction, production, and delivery to prevent products from being implanted with malware, tampered with, or forged during manufacturing.

·       **Production system security:** Establish a secure and trustworthy manufacturing and production system to defend against internal and external penetration and attacks and support continuous and reliable production.

·       **Return material and product security:** Prevent spare parts/returned materials from using forged components and prevent reused products from being implanted with malware, to secure customer data in spare parts and returned materials and ensure traceability.

## 7.3        Supply Security

·       **Anti-implantation and anti-tampering of materials:** Ensure the integrity and authenticity of products in the supply chain. Prevent the software, hardware, or data in the products produced or purchased by Huawei from being maliciously tampered with or implanted with malware during distribution, and ensure that the products delivered to customers are consistent with those released by R&D. Prevent the use of forged parts during delivery and ensure the authenticity and integrity of the products delivered to customers.

·       **Data breach prevention and return process compliance:** Process customer data or personal data contained in returned materials in accordance with legal requirements to prevent data breaches. Protect returned materials from being tampered with or forged to ensure integrity and authenticity.

## 7.4        Supply Traceability

·       **Supply traceability:** The supply chain shall establish a traceability system to support the traceability of products and parts in the supply process, produce identifiers for products and parts to facilitate tracing, and ensure that related information is effectively managed.

# 8         Delivery and Service Security

## 8.1        Customer Service Operation Security

·       **Customer authorization:** Specify the scope authorized by customers and establish a security control mechanism for obtaining and verifying customer authorization.

·       **Service tool security:** Establish a service tool management mechanism and perform routine checks.

·       **Security of operations on customer networks:** Establish processes and regulations for routine network maintenance and changes, ensure that all network operations and changes meet requirements, and keep operation records for later audit.

·       **Access and account security:** Develop principles, policies, and processes for customer network access control, and routinely manage access to customer networks on this basis.

·       **Return of spare parts:** Delete customer data from returned materials in accordance with applicable technical standards.

## 8.2        E2E Vulnerability Management

·       **Vulnerability handling preparation:** Establish a transparent and effective vulnerability handling process and responsible organization to support and protect the security and stability of customers' and Huawei's networks.

·       **Vulnerability awareness:** Establish vulnerability reporting channels and proactively monitor the supply chain or public channels to obtain comprehensive vulnerability information in a timely manner.

·       **Vulnerability validation:** Validate the obtained vulnerability information based on evidence, check the impact scope, determine the severity, and keep continuous and clear communication with stakeholders.

·       **Vulnerability remediation development:** Promptly handle confirmed vulnerabilities by impact scope to ensure product security throughout its lifecycle.

·       **Release of security bulletins:** Establish responsible vulnerability disclosure procedures and rules, collaborate with stakeholders, and provide mitigations to affected stakeholders.

·       **Remediation deployment:** Strengthen ties with the security ecosystem, encourage proactive patch management, and mitigate risks via security updates.

·       **Engagement in post-remediation activities:** Continuously improve vulnerability perception and security awareness, vulnerability management practices, and technical specifications.

# 9         Security of Huawei-Operated Businesses

## 9.1        Development and O&M Security (DevSecOps)

·       **Security of the cloud service development process:** Incorporate requirements for security design, secure coding, and security testing into the cloud service development and deployment process, and use IT tools to automate security controls.

·       **Security of cloud service products:** Provide cloud customers with access control, secure transmission, application security protection, data security, identity verification and authentication, and other capabilities.

·       **Security of cloud service O&M:** Establish a security protection system with in-depth defense and an attack response mechanism to quickly identify and mitigate attacks.

## 9.2        Business Operations Security

·       **Operations qualifications and compliance responsibilities:** Specify cyber security management organizations and owners for Huawei-operated businesses, comply with administrative licensing and archiving requirements of local laws and regulations, and build cyber security compliance management capabilities.

·       **Business risk control:** Establish a business operations security risk control mechanism based on applicable cyber security laws and regulations to prevent unauthorized use or abuse of cloud service resources.

·        **Contract agreement and responsibility boundary:** Incorporate cyber security and privacy protection clauses into cloud service product and solution sales contracts, sign service agreements with cloud service customers, and specify the cyber security and privacy protection responsibility boundary of both parties.

## 9.3        Ecosystem and Partner Security

·       **Ecosystem solution security:** Continuously gain insights into customers' security pain points and cloudification requirements, develop cyber security and privacy protection requirements for joint ecosystem solutions, build security verification capabilities, and enable ecosystem partners.

·       **App store security:** Establish a security management mechanism for the cloud store and mobile app store, specify and implement the security responsibilities of Huawei, ecosystem partners, and customers, and build E2E security management capabilities throughout the ecosystem product lifecycle from release and operations to EOM.

·       **Developer ecosystem security:** Build a security enablement system for developers and ecosystem partners, develop security courses, experiments, and training sessions, and enable developers to innovate applications based on Huawei's security architecture.

# 10     Organization and Personnel

## 10.1     Security Organization

·       **Organizations, roles, and responsibilities:** Clearly define and assign the organizations, roles, and responsibilities relevant to cyber security.

·       **Resource and competence assurance:** Provide the resources needed for the cyber security management system and ensure it has the necessary competence for relevant work.

## 10.2     Personnel Security

·       **Identification of critical positions and personnel:** Identify the cyber security risks of related positions and personnel, and establish screening criteria for personnel in critical positions.

·       **Personnel screening**: Conduct background checks on candidates for critical positions in accordance with laws, regulations, and ethical norms based on business requirements, types of accessed systems and information, and known risks.

·       **Management responsibilities:** The management shall require all employees and contractors to fulfill their security responsibilities in accordance with the established policies and processes.

·       **Reward and penalty scheme:** Establish a reward and penalty scheme familiar to employees to reward teams and employees who make outstanding contributions in cyber security work and penalize cyber security regulation violators.

·       **Position change or resignation:** Clear relevant accounts and permissions whenever an employee or contractor is transferred to another position or the contract is terminated, and notify stakeholders according to regulations.

## 10.3     Training and Education

·       **Raise the security awareness of all employees:** All employees and relevant contractors in the organization shall receive appropriate awareness training on cyber security and privacy protection.

·       **Improve awareness of the management:** Improve the security awareness of directors to make the management become aware of the importance of cyber security and privacy protection.

·       **Enablement and training for practitioners:** Provide role-based training and appraisal to ensure that cyber security practitioners have corresponding skills.

# 11     IT Platform and O&M Security

## 11.1     IT Platform and Application Security

·       **Asset management:** Manage all physical and digital assets used for product and service development and delivery, maintain the asset inventory, and specify responsibilities.

·       **Identity and access management:** Establish an access control policy based on service and security requirements to manage authorized user accounts and permissions.

·       **Certificate and key management:** Adopt appropriate and effective cryptographic techniques to protect information assets and establish a certificate and key management mechanism.

·       **Physical and environmental security:** Develop a physical security policy, establish a control mechanism, and regularly conduct physical security control activities, including physical access control, transmission media access control, and asset monitoring and tracking.

·       **Operation security:** Develop operation regulations and establish a responsibility mechanism to ensure correct and secure operation of IT infrastructure. In addition, record key activities that affect network operation. Take technical measures to record the network running status and cyber security incidents, and retain related network logs according to regulations to ensure that key network activities are recorded, traceable, and auditable.

·       **Communications security:** Ensure that information and supporting information processing facilities on networks are protected and that information is securely transmitted within the organization and between the organization and external entities.

·       **System acquisition, development, and maintenance:** Specify the security requirements of the information system and implement security development lifecycle management.

·       **Business continuity management:** Develop business continuity plans for IT infrastructure used to provide services for customers, and carry out testing and drills to continuously safeguard resilience of customer networks.

## 11.2     Data Security

·       **Data security roles and responsibilities:** Specify legal roles involved in data processing and their data security management responsibilities in accordance with applicable laws and regulations, authorization, Huawei's external commitments, and the data management policies of the domain. Set data security positions or specify the data security responsibilities of related positions based on business and process requirements.

·       **Data compliance and security requirements:** Continuously identify applicable data (including personal and non-personal data) security laws and regulations, contractual obligations, and data security requirements in external commitments to form complete data security requirements and specifications in the domain and keep them up-to-date.

·       **Management of data assets:** Based on business processes or business requirements, identify data assets, register and maintain data asset inventories and data flow diagrams/tables, perform data asset stocktaking on a regular basis or as required, and deregister the data that is no longer used.

·       **Data categorization and classification:** Establish data categorization and classification rules, classify data based on business scenarios and characteristics, determine data security levels in accordance with laws and regulations, data importance, and sensitivity, and establish a mechanism to label data based on data categorization and classification, authorization, and other elements.

·       **Data security protection:** Take appropriate management and technical measures based on data security levels to ensure the security of data assets as well as IT infrastructure and systems that carry data, and protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of data during transmission, storage, and use.

·       **Data acquisition:** Clarify the purposes of data acquisition. Obtain valid authorization prior to data collection and keep the authorization record. Typically, collect the minimum amount of data needed and ensure that data is purchased from legitimate sources. Label acquired data based on data classification and rating.

·       **Data storage:** Determine the data storage period based on external requirements (such as laws, regulations, and contracts) and the validity period of data authorization. In regions and countries where laws and regulations for local data storage are in place, store data of specified types locally as required.

·       **Data use:** Use data only for authorized purposes, control data access in compliance with the principle of least privilege, and keep data use records.

·       **Data transfer:** Before data transfer, obtain internal approval in accordance with laws and regulations, authorized scope, and business requirements; sign the Data Transfer Agreement and obtain necessary approval from statutory regulators in accordance with applicable laws and regulations; and perform risk evaluation and process data as required (such as encryption and anonymization). In addition, keep records of relevant approval and operations.

·       **Data destruction:** Manage the data storage period in strict compliance with external requirements (such as laws, regulations, and contracts) and the validity period of data authorization. Destroy expired data in a timely manner and keep the destruction records.

·       **Traceability and auditability of data activities:** Keep records of key activities such as necessary authorization for data acquisition, data access, transfer, and deletion to ensure that key data activities are recorded, traceable, and auditable.

·       **Handling of data rights requests and complaints:** Establish channels, processes, and requirements for handling data subjects' or users' rights requests and data security complaints. Respond to data subjects' requests for legal rights regarding personal data within a specified period of time. Respond to reasonable requests and complaints from non-personal data providers or other stakeholders in accordance with laws and agreements.

## 11.3     Security Threat and Incident Response

·       **Threat, vulnerability, and incident management policies and processes:** Develop threat, vulnerability, and incident management policies and processes, specify operation rules, establish relationships with customers and other external stakeholders, and communicate with or report to relevant parties according to regulations and requirements.

·       **Threat information and situational awareness:** Build the capability of collecting security threat information in the industry and of situational awareness, and identify and record internal and external cyber security threats.

·       **Vulnerability identification and risk mitigation:** Identify vulnerabilities in assets, evaluate the impacts, and implement risk mitigations.

·       **Security monitoring and anomaly detection:** Establish and manage the network operation baseline and expected data flow, and develop the detection process to detect anomalies, analyze and determine their potential impacts, and warn against incidents based on rules.

·       **Security incident response and recovery:** Establish a security incident management mechanism, develop a security incident response and recovery plan, and carry out drills. Respond to and handle security incidents and restore affected services.
